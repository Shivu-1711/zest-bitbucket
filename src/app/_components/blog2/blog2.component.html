Which is the fastest growing segment of the Fitness Industry?

Posted by Rohina Ambasat October 12, 2020 December 1, 2020 Posted in Fitness, Organic Food Tags: Fitness, gym, Gyms near me, Smartwatch, Wearable Technology

The Fitness Industry is not just about GYMS. It includes the whole lot i.e. Martial Arts Clubs, Dance Clubs, Yoga Clubs, Indoor Adventures, Swimming Pools, Gymnastics and so much more. The Industry has further diversified with its channels of delivery. It is now available not just via physical locations but also on-demand or recorded streaming of training.

The impact of technology has brought a lot of changes to the fitness industry unlike another factor.  In May, The IHRSA Global Report of May 2019, found “embracing fitness tech inside and outside the club” as the top trend followed by members belonging to multiple clubs and studios and clubs acting as social hubs for their communities1.  The global fitness app market size is poised to grow by USD 1.68 billion during 2020-2024, progressing at a CAGR of almost 12% throughout the forecast period, according to the latest report by Technavio2. Since the past few years, fitness and health clubs and suppliers have invested in a wide variety of technology, from virtual fitness solutions to AI driven solutions. This can also be viewed as part if the greater consumer shift towards better experiences rather than single transactional benefits.

Which segment is it?

The segment called Wearable Fitness! And if you want to invest in the Fitness and Recreational Industry, Wearable Fitness is your best bet. The Future Market Insights Report titled “Wearable Fitness Trackers Market: Global Industry Analysis 2012 – 2016 and Opportunity Assessment 2017 – 2027” has found that the global wearable fitness market is expected to grow continuously at a CAGR of 15.8% in terms of value during the forecast period 2017 – 2027. In Canada, Statista report on Wearable, states Canadian Wearable Revenue is expected to show an annual growth rate (CAGR 2020-2024) of -0.4%, which will result in a projected market volume of US$295m by 2024 and the user penetration will be 10.2% in 2020 and is expected to hit 8.4% by 2024.

What is Wearable Fitness?

The wearables market includes eDevices that are designed to cater for fitness. They come fitted with smart sensors and Bluetooth and a connection to the internet to measure bodily movements such as the steps taken, heartbeats, running speed, calories count, temperature, glucose measurement, sleep measurement etc. These devices are far more than a fashion statement. They can come in the form of a smart watch, Wristband, Smart shoe, Smart shirt/jacket etc.

What is the playing field like?

The major players amping-up the game are Fitbit, Garmin, Jawbone, Apple, Samsung, Fitbit, Pulsar, Sony and a lot of smaller players. Fitbit is the leading player followed by Garmin with its Vivo fitness band. There are notable small players that have unique products and technology. Interxon Inc’s Brain Sensing Headbands have found quite a few likers in the market! LVL Technologies’ is creating a LVL which is a wearable hydration monitor. Fashion meets fitness technology with Under Armour’s Gemini 2, Vitali’s Smartbra (pictured below) Sensoria’s Smart socks (Really!) and Hexoskin’s Tank.

What is the technology behind this?

The current technology being used includes computing, networking, positioning, Display and Sensor Technology. The race is all about the application of these technologies. This segment may as well see their own Tech innovation because there is a lot of demand and to move away from the clutter, serious companies will have to come up with significant differentiation.

What is causing this Demand?

There is a need for people to be have newer smart gadgets. Every new version is received with as much anticipation as the previous version. Consumers want to be trendy. Increasing health consciousness is another reason. Increase in health issues like obesity, diabetes, and other chronic diseases have created a need to collect metrics to maintain and develop a healthy lifestyle or manage and prevent chronic diseases. Good metrics will aid research. Consumers feel wearable fitness gadgets will increase the efficiency of exercise and improve quality of life.

The increase in demand for connected devices has promoted wearable fitness technology. The IOT things is really a factor where convenience is sought. Consumers want everything to be interconnected and easily accessible to make their life easier.

This is a key part of todays’ fitness culture. This will lead to adoption of technology and will be native to the current younger generation aged between 6-12 years. This will positively influence the fitness app market and have a growth trajectory4.

Impact of COVID (OH YEAH, This is the most happening blurb)

Unlike the food Industry, the consumer wearable fitness market segment was effected negatively. In September 2020 Report, Statista published new forecasts that are significantly lower, compared to 2019.  

The global wearables market is now forecast to grow by 14.5% in 2020 compared to compared to 2019, when the market grew by 89%7. COVID 19 has changed the landscape of the fitness Industry to be more digital friendly and also consumer centric. Conclusion

Is this an all happy segment? The wearable gadget market comes with its price fluctuations and data privacy concerns. Smartwatches have made in roads and is here to stay. This is largely due to the development of networking technology by large Tech companies. Internet availability has contributed to the success of wearable technology. The idea of cheaper wearable technology for children is probably will be the most useful. This segment would be the wearable technology natives! Will AI and 5G boost the competition in the market?
